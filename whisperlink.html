About WhisperLink

WhisperLink is an offline mesh communication protocol designed for device-to-device messaging without requiring internet infrastructure. The system enables resilient communication networks that operate independently of centralized servers, cell towers, or WiFi access points.

Built on principles of decentralization and privacy, WhisperLink creates ad-hoc networks where devices relay messages across the mesh until reaching their destination. This architecture ensures communication remains possible even when traditional infrastructure fails or is unavailable.

Purpose & Technology

The protocol addresses critical scenarios including emergency situations, remote areas with no connectivity, privacy-conscious communication, and situations requiring infrastructure-independent networks. WhisperLink leverages Bluetooth LE and WiFi Direct for device discovery and data transmission.

  • Zero-infrastructure mesh networking
  • End-to-end encryption by default
  • Automatic message routing and relay
  • Battery-efficient transmission protocols
  • Cross-platform compatibility (iOS, Android, desktop)
  • Offline-first architecture

Future Roadmap

Current research focuses on optimizing routing algorithms, reducing battery consumption, extending effective range through multi-hop relay, and developing cryptographic protocols that balance security with performance constraints of mobile devices.

Long-term vision includes creating a global mesh network where users contribute device capacity to a shared communication infrastructure that operates parallel to traditional internet systems.
